**Project Title**
Procurement Intelligence Dashboard
**​Description**
​A comprehensive end-to-end data visualization solution built to analyze organizational procurement patterns, track spending across departments, evaluate supplier performance, and uncover strategic cost-optimization opportunities.
​**Tech Stack**
​**Business Intelligence**: Power BI (Data Modeling, DAX, Power Query)
**​Data Processing**: SQL (for initial data extraction and transformation)
**​Data Source**
​**Procurement Enterprise Data:** Transactional dataset containing fields for Item Description, Category, Subcategory, Department, Supplier Name, Assembly Location (LAT, CHN, MEX), Quantity, Unit Cost, and Transaction Dates (2023–2024).
**​Features and Highlights**
**​Business Problem**
​Organizations often face fragmented supply chain data, making it difficult to track exactly where capital is being deployed. Without clear visibility into departmental spending, vendor concentration, and item-level costs, businesses risk overspending, relying too heavily on single suppliers, and missing critical seasonal demand patterns that impact the bottom line.
​**Goal of Dashboard**
​To provide a unified, interactive, and actionable view of procurement metrics. The dashboard is designed to empower procurement managers and stakeholders to make data-driven decisions regarding supplier diversification, budget allocation, demand forecasting, and targeted spend optimization.
**​Walkthrough of Key Visuals**
**​Spend Analysis:** A high-level executive summary displaying core KPIs (Total Spend, Total Quantity, Avg Unit Cost) alongside categorical breakdowns and departmental spending over time.
**​Assembly Demand Analysis**: Evaluates demand geographically across LAT, CHN, and MEX, plotting total quantity and spend trends by year, quarter, and month to identify regional operational needs.
​**Supplier Performance Analysis**: Ranks suppliers by total spend and volume. Includes detailed tabular views to easily identify vendor reliance (e.g., highlighting dominant suppliers vs. minor vendors).
**​Item-Level Procurement Efficiency**: Utilizes scatter plots to map the cost-volume balance across individual items, clearly delineating high-volume low-cost components from premium parts.
**​Time-Based Procurement Trend**: Tracks Year-over-Year (YoY) spend changes via a gauge chart and breaks down seasonal spending peaks by department across Q1-Q4.
**​Cost Tier Segmentation**: Analyzes transaction sizes, revealing that a large majority of transactions fall into the "High" cost tier, and identifies the key departments (Sourcing, Logistics) driving these costs.
**​Business Insights & Strategic Takeaways**
**​Spend Overview:** The organization managed a total spend of $123.15M across 630.69K units, with an average unit cost of $191.86.
**​Material & Department Trends:** Stainless Steel and Steel dominate the overall spend. The Assembly Department consistently leads in spending across all months, while Logistics and Maintenance exhibit fluctuating, variable operational needs.
**​Geographic Variances**: While LAT leads in the raw quantity of units procured (223.57K), CHN commands the highest total spend ($44.48M). Notably, MEX offers the most cost-effective sourcing with the lowest average unit cost ($182.11).
**​Critical Vendor Risk**: There is significant vendor concentration. Precision Metals accounts for over 50% of the total spend ($50.73M). Master Suppliers Ltd. charges the highest average unit cost ($452.55), indicating premium pricing.
**​Cost Segmentation**: A massive 84% of transactions fall into the High-Cost Tier, driven heavily by specific items like SS Rods (which alone account for $13.27M) and Sourcing/Logistics departments.
